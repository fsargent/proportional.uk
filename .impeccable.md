## Design Context

### Users

This site is for politically engaged UK readers who want to understand why Westminster elections produce distorted results and what better alternatives could look like. They are comparing systems, checking claims, and deciding whether the argument for changing the voting system is credible.

### Brand Personality

The tone should feel civic, trustworthy, and clear. It should build confidence through calm explanation, not hype, while still making the case that the current system produces avoidable problems.

### Aesthetic Direction

Aim for a flagship-quality public explainer with a restrained, policy-facing look. The interface should feel more like a modern civic briefing than a campaign splash page: strong hierarchy, calm colour, and clear emphasis. Avoid flashy treatments, novelty for its own sake, and anything that feels like a strict GOV.UK clone.

### Design Principles

- Put clarity before decoration.
- Use colour to guide attention, not to dramatise every section.
- Make interactive choices feel deliberate, legible, and accessible.
- Keep typography and spacing steady so the argument feels credible.
- Prefer subtle polish over conspicuous effects.
